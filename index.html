About the Disease

The Reality of ALS

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis — also called Lou Gehrig’s disease — is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that attacks the nerve cells responsible for controlling voluntary muscles in the brain and spinal cord.

The cruelest part is not the physical loss — it’s that the mind remains entirely sharp through all of it. Patients watch themselves disappear while fully awake. There is no treatment that stops it. There is no cure.

01

It takes everything

ALS destroys the nerves controlling voluntary movement. Walking, speaking, swallowing, breathing — each ability goes, one by one, while the mind remains completely intact.

02

No warning. No pattern.

90% of ALS cases have no family history. It strikes athletes, executives, parents, and children without warning. Anyone can receive the diagnosis tomorrow.

03

90 minutes

Every 90 minutes, someone in America is diagnosed with ALS. Every 90 minutes, someone dies from it. The cycle has continued for over a century.

04

Still no cure — but close

Decades of research have produced treatments that slow progression slightly. But nothing stops it. Every dollar raised funds the researchers who believe they can change that.
